Phase 0

Pre-Apprenticeship — 4 Weeks

The entry gate. No technical experience required. We build from the ground up: confidence, vocabulary, and the first conceptual breakthrough.

■

Week 1: Maker Confidence

You are not behind. You have never been behind. The AI was trained on the past — you are operating in the present. Your hands-on knowledge of real processes is an advantage the model cannot replicate. This week establishes that posture.

  • What you know that the AI doesn't
  • The confidence gap: why AI sounds certain
  • Your domain expertise is not replaceable by a model
◆

Week 2: Shop Floor Literacy

Manufacturing vocabulary, process documentation basics, and quality control fundamentals. Apprentices who understand the shop floor can write Intent Manifests that the AI cannot violate silently.

  • Reading a process specification
  • Tolerance vs. specification vs. standard
  • Quality control checkpoints and sign-off chains
⚠

Week 3: Epistemological Priming

The first hard concept: the difference between "I don't know" and "I generated a plausible answer." Why the AI cannot feel doubt. Why confidence is not a signal of accuracy. This is the conceptual foundation for everything that follows.

  • How language models generate text
  • Confabulation vs. fabrication vs. error
  • The absence of an internal error signal
⚙

Week 4: Bridge to Sovereign

Apprentices write their first Intent Manifest for a real task in their domain. They run their first probe. They score their first output. The Coherence Score becomes real — a number they computed, not a concept they heard about.

  • First Intent Manifest: your task, your domain
  • First Boundary Push probe
  • First Coherence Score computation
Phase 1

Core Intensive — 6 Weeks

The six instruments of the Sovereign curriculum. Each week is one instrument. By the end, apprentices have played all six.

WEEK 5 — THE LENS

Precision Observation

The Lens is the capacity for precise observation without interpretation. Apprentices learn to describe AI outputs exactly as they are — not as they expected, not as they wanted, not as they feared. The Lens precedes every other instrument.

WEEK 6 — THE NOISE GATE

Signal Isolation

The Noise Gate is the capacity to separate signal from noise in AI outputs. Apprentices learn to identify which parts of an AI response are relevant to the task, which are elaboration, and which are hallucination. The Gate protects the Lens from distraction.

WEEK 7 — THE PRISM

Multi-Angle Analysis

The Prism is the capacity to evaluate the same output from multiple angles simultaneously: factual accuracy, boundary compliance, intent alignment. The Prism is what makes the Coherence Score computable, not just conceptual.

WEEK 8 — THE LOGOS

Intent Architecture

The Logos is the capacity to architect intent — to specify, in advance, exactly what you want and what you don't want. The Intent Manifest is the Logos in practice. Apprentices build 10 Manifests this week, across 5 domains, calibrated against real outputs.

WEEK 9 — THE LATTICE

Pattern Memory

The Lattice is the capacity to build and use a pattern memory — a personal library of documented failure modes. Apprentices extend the Librarian Node with their own pattern library. The Lattice makes the Lattice better over time: it is self-reinforcing.

WEEK 10 — THE STANDING WAVE

Stable Governance

The Standing Wave is the integrated posture: all five instruments operating simultaneously as a stable habit. Apprentices run a full governance simulation — Intent Manifest, probe, score, decision, log — under time pressure. This is the Core Intensive exit assessment.

Phase 2

Advanced Operations — 10 Weeks

Apprentices move from controlled curriculum to live deployment. Real machines, real outputs, real stakes.

Weeks 11–16: Live Deployment

  • Embedded at IGC partner manufacturer facility (3 days/week)
  • Run live adversarial probes against production AI systems
  • Submit daily Coherence Score reports to Librarian Node
  • Weekly debrief with Sovereign Hub technical team
  • Document minimum 3 new failure mode patterns per week
  • Mentor Phase 0/1 apprentices (one session per week)

Weeks 17–20: Specialization Track

Apprentices select one specialization based on employer demand and aptitude:

  • CNC + Machining Track: Probe library for parametric machining AI
  • Quality Systems Track: Integration with ISO/AS9100 QMS documentation
  • Compliance Track: CMMC-specific governance, NIST 800-171 Au controls
  • Training Track: Become a facilitator for future cohorts
Phase 3

Strategic Governor — 12 Weeks

The apex of the program. Apprentices become Strategic Governors — not just practitioners but leaders of AI governance in their organizations.

WEEKS 21–24

Governor Dashboard Mastery

Full proficiency with the Governor Dashboard: reviewing Librarian logs, interpreting Coherence Score trends, issuing governance decisions, and producing audit packages for C3PAO assessment preparation.

WEEKS 25–28

Organizational Integration

Apprentices design and implement AI governance workflows for their employer partner. Deliverable: a complete AI Governance Plan including Intent Manifest templates, probe schedules, and escalation procedures for their specific production environment.

WEEKS 29–32

Capstone & Certification

Capstone project: a full AI Governance audit of a production system, presented to the IGC and YSU faculty panel. Successful graduates receive: "Certified Strategic Governor — Sovereign Seed Level 3" from YSU + Oak Hill. Employment pathway activated.

Employment Outcomes Target: 80% of Phase 3 graduates placed in AI Quality Controller or Strategic Governor roles at IGC partner manufacturers within 90 days of graduation. Starting wage target: $45,000–$65,000/year. This outcome data becomes the evidence base for all future grant applications.
