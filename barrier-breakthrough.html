Barrier 1

DOL Registered Apprenticeship

THE BARRIER

DOL Registration Requirement

DOL Registered Apprenticeship funding requires the program to be registered with the U.S. Department of Labor as an official Registered Apprenticeship. The registration process takes 6–18 months and requires an "apprenticeable occupation" classification — a category that does not yet exist for "AI Quality Controller."

THE BREAKTHROUGH

Logos-Aligned Solution

Register under the existing "Quality Control Technician" apprenticeable occupation (RAPIDS code 0673HY) with an AI specialization addendum. The DOL's Expanded Apprenticeship Initiative explicitly supports adding AI/emerging tech competencies to existing occupation frameworks. Begin registration immediately — the 18-month path to full funding starts now.

EV: $60K • Probability: 40% • Timeline: Month 12–18
Barrier 2

Ohio HSTI — High School Tech Internship

THE BARRIER

School District Partnership Required

Ohio High School Tech Internship Program (HSTI), operated through the Ohio BWC, requires formal partnership with a school district's Career-Technical Education (CTE) program. Without a CTE partner, the program cannot access HSTI funding.

THE BREAKTHROUGH

Logos-Aligned Solution

Approach Youngstown City Schools and Mahoning County CTC with a simple proposition: "We will build a free AI Quality Control curriculum for your students. You provide the CTE classification — we provide the instruction, the facilities, and the employer pipeline." Youngstown City Schools has active CTE programs in manufacturing — this is a natural fit.

EV: $26K • Probability: 35% • Timeline: Month 6–12
Barrier 3

DOL YouthBuild

THE BARRIER

"Disconnected Youth" Targeting Required

DOL YouthBuild funding is targeted at "disconnected youth" — ages 16–24 who are not in school and not employed. The Sovereign Apprentice Program's current recruitment pathway targets employed manufacturing workers with younger relatives, not specifically "disconnected" youth.

THE BREAKTHROUGH

Logos-Aligned Solution

Youngstown has a documented disconnected youth population that is disproportionately affected by manufacturing automation. Partner with Youngstown's YouthBuild program (already operating) to offer the Phase 0 Pre-Apprenticeship as a YouthBuild activity. Their existing case management infrastructure handles the eligibility tracking — we handle the AI curriculum.

EV: $112K • Probability: 30% • Timeline: Month 9–18
Barrier 4

McGovern Foundation Youth Component

THE BARRIER

Community Impact Demonstration

The McGovern Foundation's "AI for public purpose" grants require demonstrated community impact targeting underserved populations. The core Sovereign Seed proposal is focused on adult manufacturing workers — a strong narrative but not inherently youth-focused.

THE BREAKTHROUGH

Logos-Aligned Solution

Add the Sovereign Apprentice Program (ages 16–24) as a distinct track in the McGovern proposal. The digital equity narrative is stronger with youth: "Young people in Youngstown are being automated out of their parents' industries. We give them the skill that makes them ungovernable by the machines — they become the machines' governors instead." This is McGovern's exact language, applied precisely.

EV: $62.5K (youth component of McGovern award) • Probability: 25% • Timeline: Month 3–9
Barrier 5

Perkins V Career & Technical Education

THE BARRIER

CTE Classification and State Formula Routing

Perkins V funds flow through state formula allocations to qualifying CTE programs. The program must be classified as a CTE pathway, delivered through an eligible institution (YSU qualifies), and mapped to a recognized career cluster. "AI Quality Control" is not yet a recognized career cluster.

THE BREAKTHROUGH

Logos-Aligned Solution

Map to the existing "Information Technology" career cluster, "Network Systems" pathway (the closest existing pathway to AI governance). YSU's career services office can classify the Hallucination Audit Protocol certificate as a Perkins-eligible IT pathway credential. The classification request takes 60 days.

EV: $28K • Probability: 45% • Timeline: Month 6–12
Barrier 6

WIOA Youth Formula Funds

THE BARRIER

Workforce Development Board Routing

WIOA Youth formula funds flow through local Workforce Development Boards (WDBs). In Mahoning Valley, the WDB controls funding allocation and must approve the program as an eligible WIOA Youth activity. WDB approval requires: (a) ITA-eligible provider status, or (b) on-the-job training agreement with a specific employer.

THE BREAKTHROUGH

Logos-Aligned Solution

Route through the OJT pathway: Each IGC partner manufacturer signs an OJT agreement with the Mahoning Valley WDB for their Sovereign Apprentice Program participants. The WDB funds 50% of wages during the training period. Oak Hill provides the instruction. The manufacturer provides the worksite. Cost to manufacturer: minimal. Benefit: WIOA-funded workforce development with AI specialization.

EV: $36K • Probability: 45% • Timeline: Month 4–9
