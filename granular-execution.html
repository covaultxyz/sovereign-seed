Four Phases — Twelve Tasks — One Outcome

Granular
Execution Plan

Phase-by-phase breakdown of the Sovereign Seed execution. Every task has an owner, deadline, success metric, and contingency. Daily rhythm schedules included.

Phase 1

Foundation Lock (Tasks 1.1–1.4)

March 15–21. The week that determines everything downstream. Partner outreach and document collection.

  • 1.1
    YSU Partnership Initiation
    Duane contacts YSU department head with MOU outline. Pitch: $500K NSF, YSU as lead PI, Oak Hill does the work. YSU gets overhead, publications, and student pipeline. Request: named faculty co-investigator by March 20.
    Duane
    Mar 15–20
  • 1.2
    University of Akron Outreach
    Parallel outreach to Akron's College of Engineering. Same pitch: research partnership, curriculum co-development, regional scaling validation. If Akron declines, proceed YSU-only — do not let Akron delay NSF submission.
    Duane
    Mar 15–21
  • 1.3
    Hans LOI Distribution to 5 Manufacturers
    Hans sends LOI template to 5 specific companies. Script: "Free AI QC training for your workers. One page. You're not committing money — just stating you'd hire trained workers if available. Takes 10 minutes." Target: 3 signed by March 24.
    Hans
    Mar 15–24
  • 1.4
    WIOA Initial Contact
    You contact Ohio Governor's Office of Workforce Transformation. Email + LinkedIn. Position: "AI literacy program for 100 Ohio manufacturing workers, employer-committed pipeline." Goal: scheduled meeting in Week 2.
    You
    Mar 20
Phase 2

Submission Sprint (Tasks 2.1–2.3)

March 22–31. Document collection closes. Dossier assembly begins. The critical path narrows.

  • 2.1
    Document Collection — All Signatures
    Follow-up on all outstanding MOUs and LOIs. Hard deadline: March 27 at 5:00 PM. Duane: confirm YSU MOU signed (Akron optional but preferred). Hans: confirm 3+ LOIs signed. If YSU is delayed, escalate to department chair. Do not extend deadline — the April 3 submission is fixed.
    Duane + Hans
    Mar 22–27
  • 2.2
    Dossier Assembly
    You: Executive Summary (500 words, must include partner names and Coherence Score mention). Scouts: Technical Appendix B final draft (adversarial robustness whitepaper). You: Budget Justification in NSF format. NSF Facilities & Equipment section. All components due March 31.
    You + Scouts
    Mar 25–31
  • 2.3
    McGovern Pre-Application
    Submit McGovern Foundation pre-application (letter of inquiry, typically 2–3 pages). Frame: AI for public purpose, Youngstown-Akron corridor, digital sovereignty. This can be done in parallel with NSF — different audience, different framing, same project.
    You
    Mar 28–31
Phase 3

Pipeline Development (Tasks 3.1–3.4)

April–June 2026. NSF submitted. Now build the pipeline regardless of outcome.

  • 3.1
    ODHE Relationship Building
    Begin formal relationship with Ohio ODHE program officers. RFP expected Q2 2026. Get your project on their radar now, before the RFP drops. Position: YSU/Akron partnership, defense manufacturing focus, CMMC compliance.
    You
    Apr–May
  • 3.2
    C3PAO Partnership Formalization
    Formalize relationships with Top 3 Ohio C3PAOs (see ohio-c3paos.html). Goal: Technical Concurrence Memo upgrades to formal Memorandum of Agreement. C3PAO co-validation dramatically increases NSF 2027 competitiveness.
    Hans
    Apr–Jun
  • 3.3
    Pilot Cohort Planning
    Even without NSF award: plan pilot cohort of 20 workers using WIOA + local resources. The pilot generates the Coherence Score data and employer outcome evidence that makes every subsequent grant application stronger.
    You
    May–Jun
  • 3.4
    NSF 2027 Pre-Positioning
    If 2026 submission is rejected: immediately begin 2027 preparation. Deadline: July 21, 2026. Rejection feedback is available within 30 days. Use it. The second submission has a dramatically higher acceptance rate when the first-round feedback is incorporated.
    You
    May–Jul
Phase 4

Contingency (Tasks 4.1–4.3)

If primary paths stall. The contingency playbook is not failure — it's the pre-planned response to anticipated friction.

  • 4.1
    If YSU MOU Is Delayed Past March 27
    Escalate to YSU College of STEM Dean. Simultaneously approach Kent State University as backup academic partner. The NSF application requires ONE institutional partner — not specifically YSU. If both decline, delay submission to NSF 2027 cycle (July deadline) and use the time to build a stronger pilot.
    Duane → You
    Mar 28 trigger
  • 4.2
    If Hans Gets Fewer Than 3 LOIs
    Submit with 2 LOIs. NSF prefers 3+ but does not mandate a minimum. Include a note in the project narrative: "Additional employer partnerships in active development." Continue LOI collection post-submission — the full cohort of 3–5 can be locked before award notification.
    Hans → You
    Mar 28 trigger
  • 4.3
    If NSF 2026 Is Rejected
    Primary pivot: NSF 2027 (July 21 deadline). Secondary sustain: McGovern + WIOA provide operating capital while 2027 prep continues. Tertiary: Ohio SBA Manufacturing Upskilling ($344K, late 2026 expected RFP). The project does not die on an NSF rejection — it just shifts timelines.
    You
    Post-rejection
