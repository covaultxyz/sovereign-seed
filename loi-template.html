Artifact B — Employer Template

Employer
Letter of Intent

Template for Industrial Governance Council manufacturers. Strategic Governance, Technical Input, and Workforce Pipeline commitments. Non-binding. Supports NSF CyberAI and McGovern proposals. One page, 10 minutes.

What This Is

The LOI in Plain Language

●

What You're Signing

A letter stating that your company sees value in having workers trained on AI quality control, and that if a trained worker were available and qualified, you'd consider them for relevant positions. That's it.

⚠

What You're NOT Signing

Not a contract. Not a financial commitment. Not a guarantee of hire. The letter is non-binding. It simply says: "We support this initiative and acknowledge the need for AI-trained workers in our sector."

◆

Why It Matters

NSF requires demonstrated employer demand before funding workforce programs. Your letter is the demand signal. Without it, the grant doesn't get submitted. With it, 100 workers in your region get AI quality control training — at no cost to you.

The Template

Letter of Intent — Full Text

[DATE]

To the NSF CyberAI SFS Review Committee and Supporting Foundations:

On behalf of [Company Name], a manufacturer of [brief description — e.g., "precision machined components for defense and aerospace applications"] located in [City], Ohio, I am pleased to submit this Letter of Intent in support of the Sovereign Seed AI Workforce Initiative being led by Oak Hill Enclave in partnership with Youngstown State University and the University of Akron.

I. Strategic Governance Commitment

[Company Name] commits to designating one (1) senior production employee to participate in the Industrial Governance Council (IGC) as an employer representative. This representative will: (a) contribute domain expertise to curriculum development, (b) participate in a minimum of one (1) quarterly IGC governance meeting per year, and (c) review and provide feedback on the Hallucination Audit Protocol's relevance to our specific manufacturing processes.

II. Technical Input

We are willing to provide anonymized manufacturing process documentation for use in developing the Hallucination Audit Protocol's industry-specific probe library and Coherence Score calibration datasets. Specifically, we can provide: [choose applicable — CNC process parameters, quality control checklists, material certification workflows, assembly procedures, etc.]. All proprietary information will be anonymized before use in the curriculum.

III. Workforce Pipeline

[Company Name] anticipates a need for [number — e.g., "2–5"] workers with AI quality control competencies over the next 24 months, particularly as our CMMC 2.0 compliance requirements take effect. We commit to: (a) considering graduates of the Sovereign Seed Hallucination Audit Protocol certification for relevant positions, and (b) referring qualified employees to the program as seats become available. This commitment is non-binding and subject to our standard hiring processes and business conditions at the time of hire.

[Company Name] believes that the Sovereign Seed initiative represents a needed and pragmatic approach to AI workforce development in the Youngstown-Akron manufacturing corridor. The Hallucination Audit Protocol addresses a real and growing operational risk that our industry faces as AI systems are integrated into production processes without adequate human oversight infrastructure.

We are proud to express our support and look forward to contributing to the success of this initiative.

Respectfully,
[Name], [Title]
