Section I

Purpose & Mission

This Memorandum of Understanding establishes the framework for academic collaboration between [University Name] and Oak Hill Enclave in the development, delivery, and research evaluation of the Sovereign Seed AI Workforce Initiative — a community-anchored program building adversarial robustness capabilities in the Youngstown-Akron manufacturing corridor.

The Initiative trains factory workers to become Strategic Governors of AI systems: trained professionals who can detect hallucination, enforce intent boundaries, and maintain operational integrity in AI-assisted manufacturing processes. The curriculum aligns with CMMC 2.0 Level 2 requirements and NSF CyberAI SFS research priorities.

Section II

Core Objectives

$500K

NSF CyberAI SFS

Jointly prepare and submit a proposal for NSF CyberAI SFS Innovation Track funding. [University Name] will serve as lead Principal Investigator and submitting institution via grants.gov. Oak Hill Enclave will serve as the implementing organization.

100

Workers Trained

Co-develop and deliver the Hallucination Audit Protocol curriculum to a minimum of 100 manufacturing workers in the Youngstown-Akron corridor within 24 months of grant award. Curriculum delivered through [University Name] continuing education infrastructure.

REG.

Regional Resilience

Establish a replicable model for AI workforce development in Midwest manufacturing corridors, with documented outcomes suitable for academic publication and policy dissemination. Co-author a minimum of 2 research papers from the initiative.

Section V

Joint Outcomes & Intellectual Property

Joint Outcomes

  • NSF CyberAI SFS grant award (if funded)
  • Minimum 100 certified AI Quality Controllers
  • 2+ peer-reviewed research publications
  • Replicable curriculum licensed to additional universities
  • Coherence Score dataset (anonymized) for broader research

Intellectual Property

  • Curriculum: jointly owned. Oak Hill holds exclusive deployment rights in manufacturing sector. University holds academic publication and teaching rights.
  • Coherence Score methodology: jointly owned. Shared publication rights.
  • Sovereign Compute architecture: Oak Hill owns hardware design. Open-source documentation jointly released.
  • Research data: jointly owned. Shared access for academic purposes.
Section VI

Term & Signatures

This MOU is effective upon signature by both parties and remains in effect for 30 months (the NSF grant period plus 6 months for reporting and publication). Either party may terminate with 60 days written notice. In the event of NSF rejection, this MOU converts to an informal partnership agreement for future proposal development unless terminated by either party.
