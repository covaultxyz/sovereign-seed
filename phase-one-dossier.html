Executive Summary

The Mission Statement

Oak Hill Enclave is a community-anchored AI infrastructure initiative building adversarial robustness into the Youngstown-Akron manufacturing corridor. We train factory workers to become Strategic Governors of AI systems — not passive users, but active quality controllers who detect hallucination, enforce intent boundaries, and maintain operational integrity.

The Hallucination Audit Protocol is a 4-week curriculum delivered through Youngstown State University and the University of Akron, piloted with 3–5 manufacturers in the Hans Network's Industrial Governance Council. Graduates are certified AI Quality Controllers — a new professional category that doesn't exist yet but will be required by every CMMC-compliant defense contractor within 36 months.

We are requesting $500,000 from NSF CyberAI SFS (Innovation Track) to fund the first cohort of 100 workers, establish the Sovereign Compute Infrastructure (Brain Node + 6 Trinity Edge Nodes), and prove the model replicable across the Youngstown-Akron corridor by June 2027.

Mission Architecture

The Three-Layer Stack

Infrastructure, curriculum, and governance. Each layer is independently valuable. Together they are a defensible competitive moat.

■

Layer 1 — Sovereign Compute

Air-gapped Brain Node (Ryzen 9 7950X, Dual RTX 4090, 128GB ECC RAM, 8TB NVMe RAID) + 6 Trinity Edge Nodes (Ryzen 5, RTX 3060, 32GB each). Complete physical isolation. No cloud dependency. CMMC-ready architecture.

▲

Layer 2 — Hallucination Audit Curriculum

4-week intensive: Week 1 Baseline Establishment, Week 2 Systematic Probing, Week 3 Pattern Recognition, Week 4 Mitigation Integration. Workers graduate as certified AI Quality Controllers with documented Coherence Score competencies.

◆

Layer 3 — Intent Manifest Governance

Governor Node (human worker) → Witness Node (real-time probe) → Librarian Node (audit memory). The three-node governance architecture that operationalizes NIST SP 800-171 Rev 3 AI oversight requirements at the shop-floor level.

♬

Layer 4 — Industrial Governance Council

Manufacturer coalition that defines "AI quality" for their specific processes. The IGC is the demand side that makes the curriculum real: actual machines, actual processes, actual failure modes. Quarterly reviews.
