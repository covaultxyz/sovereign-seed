The Numerator:
Alignment

How the narrative alignment works across all funders and partners. The signal that makes every document in the dossier coherent with every other document. The alignment is not a strategy — it is a property of the truth.

The Principle

Why Alignment Is the Numerator

Every grant application has a denominator — the funder's stated priorities, the program requirements, the reviewer's checklist. Most applicants spend all their time managing the denominator: adding requirements, checking boxes, softening claims to fit the rubric.

The Sovereign Seed strategy inverts this. The numerator — what you actually do, why it actually matters, who it actually serves — is the signal. When the numerator is strong and the narrative is coherent, the denominator is just formatting. Reviewers don't fund requirements-matching. They fund projects that make them say: "This is exactly what we're trying to accomplish."

The alignment is not manufactured. The Sovereign Seed initiative genuinely addresses adversarial robustness (NSF), AI workforce development (ODHE), community AI equity (McGovern), and manufacturing employment (WIOA) — simultaneously, without contradiction. The narrative coherence is a reflection of that underlying truth.

The Signal

The Core Signal Across All Funders

One true thing, stated in five ways. Each statement is complete — not a fragment of the truth.

NSF

The Manufacturing AI Security Problem Has No Workforce Solution

The core signal for NSF CyberAI SFS reviewers

CMMC 2.0 requires defense manufacturers to govern AI systems — but no workforce training infrastructure exists. The Hallucination Audit Protocol creates that infrastructure. The Coherence Score provides a measurable, publishable methodology. The Sovereign Compute Architecture enables air-gapped deployment. This is adversarial robustness at the workforce level.

ODHE

Ohio Manufacturing Needs AI-Trained Workers — Now

The core signal for Ohio ODHE program officers

Youngstown-Akron manufacturers are adopting AI tools without any curriculum infrastructure to train workers on quality control. YSU and Akron deliver the curriculum. IGC employers hire the graduates. ODHE funds the delivery. The result: a replicable Ohio model for AI workforce development in manufacturing.

MCGOVERN

Youngstown Can Own Its AI Infrastructure — Or Surrender It

The core signal for McGovern Foundation program officers

The Sovereign Compute architecture puts AI infrastructure ownership in community hands. Workers don't become dependent on Big Tech cloud services — they become governors of locally-owned, air-gapped systems. This is digital sovereignty in practice, not in theory. McGovern's "AI for public purpose" mandate is exactly this.

WIOA

100 Ohio Manufacturing Workers. Employer-Committed Pipeline.

The core signal for WIOA Governor's Reserve

100 workers trained. 3+ manufacturers with signed LOIs committing to hire. The WIOA funding enables the training. The employer pipeline ensures the employment outcome. No theoretical pathway — actual companies, actual hiring commitments, actual workers.
