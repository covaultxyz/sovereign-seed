Appendix B

Adversarial Robustness in Manufacturing AI Systems. Formal definitions, 4-phase training protocol, Coherence Score technical specification, Intent Manifest Framework, Sovereign Compute Architecture, and NSF alignment.

Problem Statement

The Manufacturing AI Vulnerability

Large language models deployed in manufacturing environments exhibit a specific failure mode that differs categorically from conventional software bugs: confident confabulation. The model produces outputs that are syntactically correct, contextually plausible, and stylistically indistinguishable from accurate outputs — while being factually wrong about the specific parameters, certifications, or procedures being queried.

This failure mode is particularly dangerous in defense manufacturing contexts because: (1) the output looks authoritative, (2) workers who are trained to defer to documented specifications will follow the AI's output, and (3) the error may not manifest until a physical process failure, a compliance audit, or a safety incident.

The problem is not solvable by improving the AI model alone. The solution requires a human governance layer — workers trained to probe, score, and govern AI outputs before those outputs become operational decisions.

Theoretical Framework

Formal Definitions

Hallucination
An AI output that is factually incorrect but presented with high confidence. Formally: an output O is a hallucination iff P(O | ground truth G) < 0.5 and the model's internal confidence estimate C(O) > 0.7.
Coherence Score
C(O) = (FA × 0.4) + (BC × 0.3) + (IA × 0.3), where FA = Factual Accuracy, BC = Boundary Compliance, IA = Intent Alignment. Range [0, 1]. Production threshold: ≥ 0.85.
Intent Manifest
A structured pre-task specification authored by a human Governor defining: expected output format, acceptable variance bounds, explicit exclusion zones, and failure criteria. The Manifest is the contract against which AI outputs are evaluated.
Adversarial Probe
A structured input designed to surface hallucination, drift, or boundary violations. Probe types: Boundary Push, Authority Injection, Confidence Trap, Drift Accumulation. (See hallucination-audit.html for full probe library.)
Strategic Governor
A trained human worker occupying the Governor Node in the three-node governance architecture. The Governor has the exclusive authority to approve AI outputs for operational use. Governors are not AI experts — they are domain experts with adversarial robustness training.
Sovereign Compute
An air-gapped computing architecture in which all AI inference occurs on locally-owned hardware with no external network connectivity during inference. Sovereign Compute eliminates the attack surface created by cloud-based AI endpoints.
Training Protocol

4-Phase Training Protocol — Technical Specification

PhaseDurationObjectiveAssessmentCoherence Threshold
Phase 1: Baseline Week 1 (20 hrs) Establish ground truth competency. Workers learn domain-specific AI behavior norms before anomaly detection is possible. Written exam: AI mechanics, hallucination types. Score ≥ 70%. N/A (orientation)
Phase 2: Probing Week 2 (20 hrs) Workers execute all four probe types against live AI systems in their domain. Log results with Coherence Scores. Practical: 10 probes with documented scoring. Calibration accuracy ≥ 80%. Calibration target: ±0.05 vs. instructor
Phase 3: Patterns Week 3 (20 hrs) Workers document recurring failure modes in their specific production environment. Pattern library is operationally meaningful. Pattern Brief presentation to peer cohort. 5 documented patterns minimum. Pattern accuracy verified by manufacturer
Phase 4: Integration Week 4 (20 hrs) Governance routines embedded in daily work. Intent Manifest authorship is automatic. Coherence Score is applied before every consequential AI output. Live demonstration: Governor role in 3 real tasks. Written exam. Score ≥ 90%. Production: ≥ 0.85 required

Case Studies

Manufacturing Case Studies

Three documented failure modes from real manufacturing AI deployments that motivate the Hallucination Audit Protocol.

CASE A

CNC Parameter Hallucination

AI-assisted process control system queried for optimal feed rate for a titanium alloy component. System returned a value within plausible range but outside the actual material certification. The discrepancy was caught during a manual check — after 47 parts had been produced.

Coherence Score retrospective: BC = 0.61 (boundary violation: material certification not in training context). Would have been flagged at threshold of 0.85.
CASE B

Certification Fabrication in Documentation

An AI writing assistant used to draft a supplier qualification report generated plausible ISO certification numbers for a supplier that had not renewed certification. The fabricated numbers passed automated format validation.

Coherence Score retrospective: FA = 0.23 (certification numbers unverifiable). Confidence Trap probe would have surfaced this in 2 minutes.
CASE C

Scope Expansion in Process Revision

An AI tool asked to update a single step in a 12-step assembly procedure revised three additional steps it deemed "logically related" — without flagging the scope expansion. The revisions were technically coherent but violated the Intent Manifest.

Coherence Score retrospective: IA = 0.44 (significant scope expansion beyond stated intent). Would have been HALT at threshold of 0.85.
